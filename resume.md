__Jason Khadka__ obtained a BSc Physics degree from the Jacobs University Bremen, Germany and Masters of Physics degree in Complex Systems from the Institute of Cross-Disciplinary Physics and Complex Systems at Universitat de les Illes Balears, Mallorca, Spain. He worked at the Center for Free Electron Lasers with Dr. Oriol Vendrell on suppression of decoherence in two-level systems for his Bachelor’s Thesis. For his Master’s thesis, he worked on the dynamics of vascular branching morphogenesis. He also worked on the analysis of experimental data during a summer internship at the Center of Applied Space Technology and Microgravity. In 2015, he joined Dr. Karen Alim’s group at the Max Planck Institute for Dynamics and Self-Organization as a PhD student working on plant development.
